Quicksort

Quicksort is a comparison sort algorithm in the divide and conquer style. It will quickly sort large collections, on average taking O(n log n) to do it.

It will sort an array "in-place", meaning it does not need to copy elements into other arrays, and taking up more space. Quicksort can be implemented both recursively and iteratively, but the forementioned is likely the more common type, and the one used on this page.

Quicksort sections off an array in two parts by choosing a pivot point. This can be chosen randomly, but the middle element is good idea. The pivot can be seen in the animation above as the GREEN bar. After the pivot is chosen two pointers will iterate through the array from both sides of the pivot. These are seen in the animation above as the BLUE bars for the left side, and RED bars for the right side.

Iteratation continues until the index of the left pointer is no longer smaller than the index of the right pointer. During this values are swapped so that all elements on the left are smaller than the pivot point, and elements on the right are larger.

When this so-called "partitioning" is completed, a new pivot point is returned that represents either the most left or most right side of a new sub-array, which is called recursively from within the Quicksort function.
